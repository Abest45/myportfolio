1. Introduction to IFRS Tax Compliance

International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS) are designed to bring transparency, accountability, and efficiency to financial markets around the world. Tax compliance under IFRS requires understanding the specific standards that govern how taxes are reported in financial statements.

2. Key IFRS Standards for Tax

The primary standard governing tax under IFRS is IAS 12 - Income Taxes. This standard establishes how companies should account for current and future tax consequences of transactions.

3. Current Tax vs Deferred Tax

Under IAS 12, entities must distinguish between:

  • Current tax: The amount of income taxes payable (recoverable) in respect of the taxable profit (tax loss) for a period
  • Deferred tax: The estimated future tax consequences of transactions and events recognized in the financial statements of the current and previous periods

4. Recognition of Deferred Tax Assets

A deferred tax asset should be recognized for deductible temporary differences to the extent that it is probable that taxable profit will be available against which the deductible temporary difference can be utilized.

5. Disclosure Requirements

IFRS requires comprehensive disclosures about tax, including:

  • Major components of tax expense
  • Explanation of the relationship between tax expense and accounting profit
  • Details of deferred tax assets and liabilities
  • Tax uncertainties and provisions

6. Recent Updates and Changes

The IFRS tax landscape continues to evolve with new interpretations and amendments. Recent changes include updated guidance on uncertain tax treatments and the implementation of the OECD's BEPS (Base Erosion and Profit Shifting) initiatives.
